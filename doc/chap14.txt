  
  [1X14 [33X[0;0YLibrary of predefined surfaces[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YWhile  chapter  [14X4[114X  described how to construct polygonal complexes from their
  incidence  structure,  this  chapter describes how to access the pre-defined
  surfaces and complexes in this package.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YThe library serves two main purposes:[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6YIt  is  a collection of a large number of polygonal complexes that can
        be  used  to test theories. This collection will change over time when
        new  surfaces are added. The collection can be accessed in this way by
        methods like [9XAllSimplicialSurfaces[109X ([14X14.1-1[114X).[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6YFor  certain  classes of polygonal complexes (like simplicial spheres)
        it  provides  a complete classification (within certain bounds). These
        lists  are  complete  and  can  be  the  basis  for theorems. They are
        presented in section [14X14.2[114X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YSection [14X14.1[114X explains the main syntax that is used to access the library. It
  also contains the methods to access the complete collection of complexes.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YSection  [14X14.2[114X  covers  the  specific classes of polygonal complexes that are
  (fully) contained in the package.[133X
  
  
  [1X14.1 [33X[0;0YAccessing all stored complexes[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YThis  section  explains  how  all polygonal complexes that are stored in the
  library    can    be    accessed.    This    is    done    by   the   method
  [9XAllTwistedPolygonalComplexes[109X   ([14X14.1-1[114X),  but  it  can  also  be  restricted
  further, for example by [9XAllSimplicialSurfaces[109X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YThe syntax of this call can be a bit confusing at first, since it was chosen
  to  give  the  user much flexibility. We will start with a few examples. The
  call[133X
  
  [4X[32X  Example  [32X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XAllSimplicialSurfaces( NumberOfFaces, 3 );[127X[104X
    [4X[28X[ simplicial surface (3 vertices, 6 edges, and 3 faces)  ][128X[104X
  [4X[32X[104X
  
  [33X[0;0Yreturns  all  simplicial  surfaces  from the library that have exactly three
  faces.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YIf we wanted all simplicial surfaces with 6, 8, or 9 edges, we could use[133X
  
  [4X[32X  Example  [32X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XAllSimplicialSurfaces( NumberOfEdges, [6,8,9] );[127X[104X
    [4X[28X[ simplicial surface (3 vertices, 9 edges, and 6 faces),[128X[104X
    [4X[28X  simplicial surface (4 vertices, 6 edges, and 4 faces), [128X[104X
    [4X[28X  simplicial surface (3 vertices, 6 edges, and 3 faces), [128X[104X
    [4X[28X  simplicial surface (3 vertices, 6 edges, and 4 faces) ][128X[104X
  [4X[32X[104X
  
  [33X[0;0YIt  is even possible to combine these restrictions. To obtain all simplicial
  surfaces with 6 edges and 4 faces, we could use[133X
  
  [4X[32X  Example  [32X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XAllSimplicialSurfaces(NumberOfEdges, 6, NumberOfFaces, [4]);[127X[104X
    [4X[28X[ simplicial surface (4 vertices, 6 edges, and 4 faces), [128X[104X
    [4X[28X  simplicial surface (3 vertices, 6 edges, and 4 faces) ][128X[104X
  [4X[32X[104X
  
  [33X[0;0YIn  general,  the  arguments  of  [9XAllSimplicialSurfaces[109X  alternate between a
  function  and its result (or list of possible results). The strength of this
  syntax  derives  from its versatility - it is actually possible to use every
  [13Xconceivable[113X  function.  Obviously,  we  can  use  functions  defined by this
  package. For example, the following command returns all predefined tori:[133X
  
  [4X[32X  Example  [32X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XAllPolygonalComplexes( IsConnectedComplex, true, [127X[104X
    [4X[25X>[125X [27X           IsOrientableComplex, true, EulerCharacteristic, 0 );[127X[104X
    [4X[28X[ simplicial surface (3 vertices, 9 edges, and 6 faces), [128X[104X
    [4X[28X  simplicial surface (9 vertices, 27 edges, and 18 faces), [128X[104X
    [4X[28X  simplicial surface (4 vertices, 12 edges, and 8 faces) ][128X[104X
  [4X[32X[104X
  
  [33X[0;0YSince  it  is  tedious to always write [9Xtrue[109X, there is a shortcut implemented
  that interprets "missing" results as [9Xtrue[109X:[133X
  
  [4X[32X  Example  [32X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XAllPolygonalComplexes( IsConnectedComplex, IsOrientableComplex, EulerCharacteristic, 0 );[127X[104X
    [4X[28X[ simplicial surface (3 vertices, 9 edges, and 6 faces), [128X[104X
    [4X[28X  simplicial surface (9 vertices, 27 edges, and 18 faces), [128X[104X
    [4X[28X  simplicial surface (4 vertices, 12 edges, and 8 faces) ][128X[104X
  [4X[32X[104X
  
  [33X[0;0YIf this is not sufficient, any user can define their own functions to search
  for   specific   surfaces.  For  example,  if  one  was  interested  in  all
  non-orientable simplicial surfaces such that one vertex in incident to every
  face, we could find them like this:[133X
  
  [4X[32X  Example  [32X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XHasCentralVertex := function(surface)[127X[104X
    [4X[25X>[125X [27X       local intersect;[127X[104X
    [4X[25X>[125X [27X       intersect := Intersection(VerticesOfFaces(surface));[127X[104X
    [4X[25X>[125X [27X       return Length(intersect) > 0;[127X[104X
    [4X[25X>[125X [27Xend;[127X[104X
    [4X[28Xfunction( surface ) ... end[128X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XAllSimplicialSurfaces(IsOrientableSurface, false, HasCentralVertex);[127X[104X
    [4X[28X[ simplicial surface (3 vertices, 6 edges, and 3 faces), [128X[104X
    [4X[28X  simplicial surface (3 vertices, 6 edges, and 4 faces), [128X[104X
    [4X[28X  simplicial surface (4 vertices, 12 edges, and 8 faces) ][128X[104X
  [4X[32X[104X
  
  [1X14.1-1 AllTwistedPolygonalComplexes[101X
  
  [33X[1;0Y[29X[2XAllTwistedPolygonalComplexes[102X( [3Xfct1[103X, [3Xres1[103X, [3Xfct2[103X, [3Xres2[103X, [3X...[103X ) [32X function[133X
  [33X[1;0Y[29X[2XAllPolygonalComplexes[102X( [3Xfct1[103X, [3Xres1[103X, [3Xfct2[103X, [3Xres2[103X, [3X...[103X ) [32X function[133X
  [33X[1;0Y[29X[2XAllTriangularComplexes[102X( [3Xfct1[103X, [3Xres1[103X, [3Xfct2[103X, [3Xres2[103X, [3X...[103X ) [32X function[133X
  [33X[1;0Y[29X[2XAllPolygonalSurfaces[102X( [3Xfct1[103X, [3Xres1[103X, [3Xfct2[103X, [3Xres2[103X, [3X...[103X ) [32X function[133X
  [33X[1;0Y[29X[2XAllSimplicialSurfaces[102X( [3Xfct1[103X, [3Xres1[103X, [3Xfct2[103X, [3Xres2[103X, [3X...[103X ) [32X function[133X
  [6XReturns:[106X  [33X[0;10Ya list of polygonal complexes[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YReturn  all  twisted polygonal complexes that are stored in the library with
  the desired properties.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YThe  number  of  arguments can be arbitrarily long. In general the arguments
  have  to  come in pairs. The first argument in such a pair is a function and
  the  second  argument  is  either  the  result of that function or a list of
  accepted results. For example[133X
  
  [4X[32X  Example  [32X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XAllTwistedPolygonalComplexes( NumberOfVertices, [10,12], IsOrientableComplex, false );[127X[104X
  [4X[32X[104X
  
  [33X[0;0Yreturns  all  non-orientable  twisted  polygonal  complexes  with  10  or 12
  vertices from the library.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YTo obtain the platonic solids a custom function has to be written:[133X
  
  [4X[32X  Example  [32X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XDegreeRegular := function( complex )[127X[104X
    [4X[25X>[125X [27X     local degrees;[127X[104X
    [4X[25X>[125X [27X [127X[104X
    [4X[25X>[125X [27X     degrees := Set(FaceDegreesOfVertices(complex));[127X[104X
    [4X[25X>[125X [27X     return Size(degrees) = 1 and degrees[1] >= 3;[127X[104X
    [4X[25X>[125X [27X   end;;[127X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27Xplat := AllPolygonalComplexes( IsPolygonalSurface, true,[127X[104X
    [4X[25X>[125X [27X     EulerCharacteristic, 2, DegreeRegular, true,[127X[104X
    [4X[25X>[125X [27X     IsConnectedSurface, true, IsClosedSurface, true);;[127X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XSize(plat);[127X[104X
    [4X[28X5[128X[104X
  [4X[32X[104X
  
  [33X[0;0YFor user convenience the following simplifications may be used:[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6YIf  one  of the more specific incarnations, like [9XAllSimplicialSurfaces[109X
        is  called,  the  returned  complexes  will  satisfy  this  additional
        requirement (in this case, they will be simplicial surfaces).[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6YIf  the  function  in  the first argument pair is [9XNrOfFaces[109X, it can be
        omitted. This is the only possible case in which the first argument is
        not a function.[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6YIf the second argument of a pair is omitted, it is assumed to be [9Xtrue[109X.
        This is especially useful if the corresponding function is a property.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YFor  example, the above command for the platonic solids can be simplified as
  follows:[133X
  
  [4X[32X  Example  [32X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27Xplat := AllPolygonalSurfaces( EulerCharacteristic, 2, DegreeRegular,[127X[104X
    [4X[25X>[125X [27X     IsConnectedSurface, IsClosedSurface );;[127X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XSize(plat);[127X[104X
    [4X[28X5[128X[104X
  [4X[32X[104X
  
  [33X[0;0YTo get just the platonic solids with triangular faces, the following command
  may be used:[133X
  
  [4X[32X  Example  [32X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27Xplat := AllSimplicialSurfaces( EulerCharacteristic, 2, DegreeRegular,[127X[104X
    [4X[25X>[125X [27X     IsConnectedSurface, IsClosedSurface );;[127X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XSize(plat);[127X[104X
    [4X[28X3[128X[104X
  [4X[32X[104X
  
  [33X[0;0YTo  obtain  just  tetrahedron  and  octahedron,  the  number of faces can be
  restricted:[133X
  
  [4X[32X  Example  [32X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27Xplat := AllPolygonalComplexes( [4,8], [127X[104X
    [4X[25X>[125X [27X              EulerCharacteristic, 2, IsConnectedComplex, IsClosedComplex );;[127X[104X
    [4X[25Xgap>[125X [27XSize(plat);[127X[104X
    [4X[28X2[128X[104X
  [4X[32X[104X
  
  
  [1X14.2 [33X[0;0YAccessing the classifications[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YIn  this  section,  different  classifications  of  polygonal  complexes are
  described.  In most cases, these are infinite families. The documentation of
  each  method  explains  in  detail  which  part of the classification can be
  relied upon.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YThe general syntax is identical to the one from [9XAllTwistedPolygonalComplexes[109X
  or [9XAllSimplicialSurfaces[109X ([14X14.1-1[114X).[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YCurrently, the following classes are contained in the package:[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6Y[9XAllPlatonicSurfaces[109X ([14X14.3-1[114X): all platonic solids[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6Y[9XAllSimplicialSpheres[109X  ([14X14.2-1[114X):  simplicial  spheres without 3--waists
        (i.e.  each  of  their  vertex-edge-paths  ([14X8.1[114X)  of  length  3 is the
        perimeter path ([14X8.2[114X) of a face).[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6Y[9XAllGeodesicSelfDualSurfaces[109X ([14X14.2-2[114X): geodesic self-dual surfaces[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YTODO needs nicer introduction and streamlining[133X

  [33X[1;0Y[29X[2XAllSimplicialSpheres[102X( [3Xfct1[103X, [3Xres1[103X, [3Xfct2[103X, [3Xres2[103X, [3X...[103X ) [32X function[133X
  [6XReturns:[106X  [33X[0;10Ya list of simplicial surfaces[133X
