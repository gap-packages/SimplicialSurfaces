  
  [1X5 [33X[0;0YAccess to twisted polygonal complexes[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YIn  section  [14X2.2[114X  we  introduced the concept of [9XTwistedPolygonalComplex[109X that
  went  further  than  a  pure incidence geometry. This chapter describes this
  additional structure and how to access it.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YSection  [14X5.1[114X  covers  the extension of the incidence structure for polygonal
  complexes  (compare  Section  [14X3.2[114X)  to  chambers.  Section  [14X5.2[114X  covers  the
  adjacency  structure  of  those chambers. Finally, Section [14X5.3[114X presents some
  methods  to  construct  twisted  polygonal  complexes.  Finally, Section [14X5.4[114X
  explores  the  relation  between  polygonal  complexes and twisted polygonal
  complexes.[133X
  
  
  [1X5.1 [33X[0;0YChamber incidence[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YPolygonal complexes only focus on the incidence between vertices, edges, and
  faces.  In  contrast,  twisted polygonal complexes also care about [13Xchambers[113X,
  which  can  be  interpreted  as  the  "small  triangles"  in  a  barycentric
  subdivision.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YEach  of these chambers is incident to exactly one vertex, one edge, and one
  face.  Thus,  we  can  use  *Of*-methods,  similar  as in Section [14X3.2[114X. As an
  example,  we  use  the following twisted polygonal complex: Image omitted in
  terminal text[133X
